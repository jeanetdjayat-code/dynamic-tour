Discover China's Fairyland of Turquoise Lakes and Waterfalls

Discover Jiuzhaigou

Jiuzhaigou National Park, located in northern Sichuan Province, is one of China's most spectacular and beloved natural destinations. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its stunning turquoise and emerald-colored lakes, cascading waterfalls, snow-capped mountain peaks, and ancient forests. The park's name means "Valley of Nine Villages," referring to the nine Tibetan villages scattered throughout the valley. Jiuzhaigou's unique karst landscape, combined with the mineral-rich waters that create the park's famous multi-colored lakes, makes it one of the most beautiful places on Earth. The park offers breathtaking hiking trails, scenic bus routes, and the chance to experience Tibetan culture and the region's incredible biodiversity.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 2-3 days

Best Time to Visit: September to October (autumn) when the leaves turn golden and red, creating a spectacular contrast with the turquoise lakes. Spring (April to May) offers beautiful wildflowers, while summer is lush and green. Winter (December to February) offers a magical snow-covered landscape with frozen waterfalls and fewer crowds.

Five Flower Lake

Jiuzhaigou's most famous and iconic lake, often called the "Jewel of Jiuzhaigou," renowned for its incredible array of colors from turquoise to sapphire.

The lake's vibrant colors are created by mineral deposits, aquatic plants, and algae reacting with sunlight. Visitors can walk along wooden boardwalks surrounding the lake.

Long Lake

Jiuzhaigou's largest, deepest, and highest-altitude lake at over 3,100 meters, stretching 7 kilometers with crystal clear waters.

Especially beautiful in winter when it freezes over completely, and in summer when the lake reflects surrounding snow-capped mountains and forests.

Multi-Color Pond

One of Jiuzhaigou's smallest but most spectacular lakes, featuring water that changes colors dramatically from turquoise to golden hues.

Visitors can walk along elevated wooden walkways circling the pond, with water so clear that ancient tree trunks at the bottom are perfectly visible.

Nuorilang Waterfall

Jiuzhaigou's most spectacular waterfall, stretching 270 meters wide and dropping 24.5 meters, the widest high-altitude waterfall in China.

Visitors can view the waterfall from multiple perspectives including the cliff top and below. In winter, it transforms into a stunning frozen spectacle.

Shuzheng Lakes

A beautiful chain of 19 interconnected lakes cascading down a mountain valley, varying in size, shape, and color from turquoise to emerald.

Visitors can explore via well-maintained wooden boardwalks, with highlights including the 20-meter-high multi-tiered Shuzheng Waterfall.

Local Food

Jiuzhaigou offers a unique blend of Tibetan and Sichuan cuisine, featuring hearty mountain dishes, dairy products, and bold flavors.

Tibetan Yak Hotpot

A must-try specialty featuring tender yak meat cooked in rich broth with various mushrooms, wild vegetables, and Tibetan herbs.

Tibetan Butter Tea

Traditional staple beverage made from yak butter, tea leaves, and salt, providing essential calories for high-altitude cold climate.

Tsampa

Roasted barley flour, a staple food mixed with butter tea, yak butter, and sometimes cheese or sugar to create nourishing food.

Tibetan Hand-Pulled Noodles

Thick, chewy handmade noodles served in savory broth with meat, vegetables, and spices, especially comforting in cool mountain climate.
