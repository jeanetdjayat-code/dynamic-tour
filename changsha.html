Changsha

Explore the capital of Hunan Province, known for its spicy cuisine and rich cultural heritage

Discover Changsha

Changsha, the capital of Hunan Province, is a vibrant city with a history dating back over 3,000 years. It's known for its spicy Hunan cuisine, rich cultural heritage, and modern development. The city is home to several must-visit attractions that showcase its unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 2-3 days

Best Time to Visit: April to June (spring to early summer) when the weather is pleasant, or September to November (autumn) when the temperatures are mild.

Orange Isle

Orange Isle is a long, narrow island in the middle of the Xiang River. It's known for its beautiful scenery, particularly the cherry blossoms in spring and the lotus flowers in summer. The island is home to a large statue of Chairman Mao Zedong, which is one of the most iconic landmarks in Changsha.

Visitors can take a walk along the island's scenic paths, rent bicycles, or take a boat ride on the river. It's a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Changsha.

Yuelu Mountain

Yuelu Mountain is located on the west bank of the Xiang River. It's known for its beautiful natural scenery and historical sites, including the Yuelu Academy, one of the four great academies of ancient China.

The mountain offers hiking trails that lead to various temples, pavilions, and scenic viewpoints. At the top, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Changsha city. It's a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and connect with nature.

Changsha IFS

Changsha IFS (International Finance Square) is a modern shopping mall and skyscraper complex in the heart of Changsha. It's one of the city's most iconic modern landmarks, featuring a large art installation of two giant pandas climbing the building.

The mall houses a variety of international brands, restaurants, and entertainment options. It's a popular destination for shopping, dining, and experiencing modern Changsha. The rooftop garden offers stunning views of the city and is a great spot for photos.

Hunan Provincial Museum

The Hunan Provincial Museum is one of the most important museums in China. It houses a vast collection of artifacts from Hunan's history, including the famous Mawangdui Han Dynasty tombs artifacts.

The museum's exhibits showcase the rich cultural heritage of Hunan Province, from ancient times to the present. It's a must-visit for anyone interested in Chinese history and culture, offering a deep insight into the region's past.

Local Food

Hunan cuisine is known for its bold, spicy flavors, and Changsha is the perfect place to experience it. The local dishes are characterized by their use of chili peppers, garlic, and fermented ingredients.

Stinky Tofu

A popular street food in Changsha, stinky tofu is fermented tofu that's fried until crispy. It has a strong odor but a delicious, spicy flavor that's beloved by locals.

Chairman Mao's Braised Pork

A famous Hunan dish, this braised pork is tender and flavorful, with a rich, savory sauce. It's said to be a favorite of Chairman Mao Zedong, who was from Hunan.

Spicy Crayfish

A popular summer dish in Changsha, spicy crayfish is cooked with chili peppers, garlic, and other spices. It's typically served with beer and is a favorite among locals.

Rice Noodles

Changsha-style rice noodles are typically served in a spicy broth with various toppings, such as beef, pork, or vegetables. They're a popular breakfast dish in the city.
