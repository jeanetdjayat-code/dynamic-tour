Zhangjiajie

Discover the Real Avatar Mountains - China's Most Spectacular Natural Wonder

Discover Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is one of China's most breathtaking natural destinations, famous for its towering sandstone pillars, dramatic mountain landscapes, and pristine wilderness. Located in Hunan Province, this UNESCO World Heritage Site served as inspiration for the floating mountains in James Cameron's film "Avatar." The region features thousands of towering quartz sandstone pillars, some reaching heights of over 300 meters, creating a surreal, otherworldly landscape that attracts millions of visitors each year. Zhangjiajie offers stunning hiking trails, glass bridges, cable car rides, and the chance to experience the incredible biodiversity of one of China's most beautiful natural regions.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 3-4 days

Best Time to Visit: March to May (spring) and September to November (autumn) when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its best. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter offers beautiful snow-covered landscapes but can be quite cold. The park is especially beautiful in the early morning when clouds and mist create a magical atmosphere.

Main Tourist Attractions

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is China's first national forest park and the crown jewel of Zhangjiajie's natural wonders. This UNESCO World Heritage Site covers over 4,800 hectares and is famous for its thousands of towering quartz sandstone pillars, some reaching heights of over 300 meters. The park served as the inspiration for the floating "Hallelujah Mountains" in James Cameron's film "Avatar," bringing worldwide fame to this spectacular natural landscape.

The park features multiple scenic areas including Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mountain, and Golden Whip Stream, each offering unique perspectives of the magical pillar forest. Visitors can explore via well-maintained hiking trails, scenic boardwalks, cable cars, and the famous Bailong Elevator - the world's tallest outdoor elevator. The park's surreal landscape, with pillars piercing through clouds and mist, creates an otherworldly atmosphere that has made it one of China's most beloved natural destinations.

Tianmen Mountain National Forest Park

Tianmen Mountain National Forest Park is a spectacular natural wonder centered around the majestic 1,518-meter Tianmen Mountain. The mountain is famous for its massive natural archway known as Tianmen Cave, or "Heaven's Gate," which stands an impressive 131.5 meters high and was formed through centuries of natural erosion. The park is accessible via the world's longest passenger cable car, a 7.5-kilometer journey that offers breathtaking aerial views during the ascent.

Visitors can climb the legendary 999 "Heavenly Ladder" steps up to the cave, or take the modern escalator system for a more comfortable ascent. The mountain features thrilling cliffside glass walkways, beautiful temples, the famous 99-turn "Avenue to Heaven" winding road, and panoramic views of the surrounding region. Tianmen Mountain offers a different but equally awe-inspiring experience compared to the quartz sandstone pillars, showcasing Zhangjiajie's diverse natural beauty.

Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon is a stunning natural gorge featuring dramatic cliffs, crystal-clear streams, and lush vegetation. The canyon offers a different experience from the famous quartz sandstone pillars, showcasing Zhangjiajie's diverse geological features. The highlight of the canyon is the world-famous Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge, which spans 430 meters across the canyon at a height of 300 meters, holding the Guinness World Record as the longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge in the world.

Visitors can explore the canyon via scenic hiking trails that wind past waterfalls, natural pools, and beautiful rock formations. The canyon also offers exciting activities including bungee jumping from the glass bridge, zip-lining across the canyon, and boat rides on the peaceful lower section. The combination of natural beauty and thrilling man-made attractions makes Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon a must-visit destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike.

Yellow Dragon Cave

Yellow Dragon Cave is one of China's most spectacular and largest caves, often called the "Underground Magical Palace." This massive limestone cave system stretches for over 15 kilometers and features four levels, including underground rivers, lakes, and thousands of stunning stalactites and stalagmites. The cave gets its name from a legend about a yellow dragon that once inhabited the cave, adding a touch of mystery to this natural wonder.

Visitors can explore the cave via well-lit walkways and boardwalks, passing through magnificent caverns adorned with intricate rock formations that have been forming for millions of years. The cave features several iconic landmarks including the "Dragon King's Palace," "Waterfall Hall," and the famous "Needle of the Gods" - a slender stalagmite that stands nearly 20 meters tall. A boat ride on the underground river offers a unique perspective of this subterranean wonderland, making Yellow Dragon Cave a fascinating complement to Zhangjiajie's outdoor scenery.

Baofeng Lake

Baofeng Lake is a beautiful alpine lake nestled among towering sandstone peaks, offering a serene and picturesque contrast to Zhangjiajie's dramatic mountain landscapes. This man-made lake, created by damming a valley, features crystal-clear emerald waters that perfectly reflect the surrounding cliffs and forested peaks. The lake is surrounded by lush vegetation and offers a peaceful escape from the busier areas of Zhangjiajie.

The most popular way to experience Baofeng Lake is by taking a traditional Chinese-style boat tour, which glides across the tranquil waters while guides share local legends and point out interesting rock formations. Visitors can also hike around the lake's perimeter, enjoying scenic views and spotting wildlife. The lake is especially famous for the "Baofeng Waterfall" that cascades down the cliffs into the lake, and the charming "Yingwo Village" perched on the mountainside. Baofeng Lake offers a peaceful, romantic experience that captures the essence of Zhangjiajie's natural beauty.

Local Food

Zhangjiajie offers a delicious blend of Hunan and local mountain cuisine, featuring fresh local ingredients, bold flavors, and unique cooking methods. The region's mountainous terrain provides an abundance of fresh wild vegetables, mushrooms, and bamboo that are featured in many local dishes.

Zhangjiajie Smoked Pork

A local specialty, Zhangjiajie smoked pork is made from free-range mountain pigs, cured with salt and spices, then smoked over pine branches and tea leaves for weeks. The result is tender, flavorful meat with a distinctive smoky aroma. It's often stir-fried with wild mountain vegetables like bracken fern or bamboo shoots, creating a perfect combination of smoky, savory flavors that represent the mountain region's culinary heritage.

Wild Mountain Vegetables

Zhangjiajie's mountainous region is rich in wild edible plants and mushrooms. Local cuisine features various wild mountain vegetables including bracken fern (juecai), wild bamboo shoots, mushroom varieties, and edible fungi. These fresh, organic ingredients are often stir-fried with garlic and chili, or served in hotpot, offering a unique taste of the region's natural bounty. The crisp texture and earthy flavors make these dishes a favorite among visitors seeking authentic mountain cuisine.

Hehua Fish (Lotus Flower Fish)

Hehua Fish is a Zhangjiajie specialty named after its delicate, flower-like presentation. The dish features whole fish, typically carp, scored and fried until golden and crispy, then served with a sweet and sour sauce. The preparation creates a beautiful "lotus flower" shape when the fish fans out during frying. The crispy exterior and tender flesh, combined with the tangy sauce, create a delicious balance of textures and flavors that's been a local favorite for generations.

Zhangjiajie Rice Noodles

Zhangjiajie is famous for its delicious rice noodles, a popular breakfast and street food option. The fresh, chewy rice noodles are served in a rich, flavorful broth with various toppings including braised pork, pickled vegetables, peanuts, and fresh herbs. Local variations include spicy versions with chili oil, as well as soup-based and stir-fried options. Many visitors consider Zhangjiajie's rice noodles among the best in Hunan Province, with a texture and flavor that's uniquely satisfying.
