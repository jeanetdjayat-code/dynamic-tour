Discover the Real Avatar Mountains - China's Most Spectacular Natural Wonder

Discover Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is one of China's most breathtaking natural destinations, famous for its towering sandstone pillars, dramatic mountain landscapes, and pristine wilderness. Located in Hunan Province, this UNESCO World Heritage Site served as inspiration for the floating mountains in James Cameron's film "Avatar." The region features thousands of towering quartz sandstone pillars, some reaching heights of over 300 meters, creating a surreal, otherworldly landscape that attracts millions of visitors each year. Zhangjiajie offers stunning hiking trails, glass bridges, cable car rides, and the chance to experience the incredible biodiversity of one of China's most beautiful natural regions.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 3-4 days

Best Time to Visit: March to May (spring) and September to November (autumn) when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its best. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter offers beautiful snow-covered landscapes but can be quite cold. The park is especially beautiful in the early morning when clouds and mist create a magical atmosphere.

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

China's first national forest park and UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for thousands of towering quartz sandstone pillars that inspired the "Hallelujah Mountains" in the film Avatar.

The park features multiple scenic areas including Yuanjiajie, Tianzi Mountain, and Golden Whip Stream, accessible via hiking trails, cable cars, and the famous Bailong Elevator.

Tianmen Mountain National Forest Park

A spectacular natural wonder centered around the 1,518-meter Tianmen Mountain, famous for its massive natural archway known as Tianmen Cave, or "Heaven's Gate."

Accessible via the world's longest passenger cable car, the mountain features thrilling cliffside glass walkways, the 999 "Heavenly Ladder" steps, and the famous 99-turn winding road.

Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon

A stunning natural gorge featuring dramatic cliffs, crystal-clear streams, and lush vegetation, offering a different experience from the famous quartz sandstone pillars.

The highlight is the world-famous Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge, spanning 430 meters across the canyon at 300 meters high, holding the Guinness World Record for the longest glass-bottomed bridge.

Yellow Dragon Cave

One of China's most spectacular caves, often called the "Underground Magical Palace." This massive limestone cave system stretches over 15 kilometers with four levels of underground rivers and lakes.

Visitors can explore via well-lit walkways, passing magnificent caverns adorned with intricate stalactites and stalagmites that have been forming for millions of years.

Baofeng Lake

A beautiful alpine lake nestled among towering sandstone peaks, offering a serene and picturesque contrast to Zhangjiajie's dramatic mountain landscapes.

The most popular way to experience the lake is by taking a traditional Chinese-style boat tour, gliding across tranquil waters while guides share local legends.

Local Food

Zhangjiajie offers a delicious blend of Hunan and local mountain cuisine, featuring fresh local ingredients, bold flavors, and unique cooking methods.

Zhangjiajie Smoked Pork

A local specialty made from free-range mountain pigs, cured with salt and spices, then smoked over pine branches for weeks.

Wild Mountain Vegetables

Rich in wild edible plants like bracken fern, wild bamboo shoots, and mushrooms, often stir-fried with garlic and chili.

Hehua Fish (Lotus Flower Fish)

Named for its flower-like presentation, featuring whole fish scored and fried until golden, served with sweet and sour sauce.

Zhangjiajie Rice Noodles

Famous fresh rice noodles served in rich broth with toppings like braised pork, pickled vegetables, peanuts, and herbs.
