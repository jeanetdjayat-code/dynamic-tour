Zhangjiajie

Discover the Real Avatar Mountains - China's Most Spectacular Natural Wonder

Discover Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie is one of China's most breathtaking natural destinations, famous for its towering sandstone pillars, dramatic mountain landscapes, and pristine wilderness. Located in Hunan Province, this UNESCO World Heritage Site served as inspiration for the floating mountains in James Cameron's film "Avatar." The region features thousands of towering quartz sandstone pillars, some reaching heights of over 300 meters, creating a surreal, otherworldly landscape that attracts millions of visitors each year. Zhangjiajie offers stunning hiking trails, glass bridges, cable car rides, and the chance to experience the incredible biodiversity of one of China's most beautiful natural regions.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 3-4 days

Best Time to Visit: March to May (spring) and September to November (autumn) when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its best. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter offers beautiful snow-covered landscapes but can be quite cold. The park is especially beautiful in the early morning when clouds and mist create a magical atmosphere.

Main Tourist Attractions

Tianzi Mountain

Tianzi Mountain is one of Zhangjiajie's most iconic and breathtaking destinations, featuring towering sandstone pillars that rise dramatically from the valley floor. The mountain is named after a local Tujia ethnic hero and offers some of the most spectacular views in the entire park. The mountain's famous "Avatar Mountains" are clustered here, with over 3,000 towering quartz sandstone pillars creating an otherworldly landscape.

Visitors can take a cable car up to the mountain's summit, where they'll be rewarded with panoramic views of the magnificent pillar forest. The area features several scenic viewing platforms, including the famous Imperial Brush Peak and Fairy Spreading Flowers. Tianzi Mountain is especially magical during sunrise and sunset when the light creates stunning shadows and colors across the landscape. The mountain also offers beautiful hiking trails through the ancient pine forests and along the cliff edges.

Yuanjiajie Scenic Area

Yuanjiajie is the most famous scenic area in Zhangjiajie, renowned as the real-life inspiration for the floating Hallelujah Mountains in the film "Avatar." This stunning area features the tallest and most dramatic sandstone pillars in the park, including the famous "Southern Sky Column" which stands an impressive 1,080 meters tall. The area became world-famous after the release of the blockbuster film, drawing visitors from around the globe.

Visitors can explore Yuanjiajie via a series of well-maintained boardwalks that wind along the cliff edges, offering breathtaking views at every turn. The scenic area features several iconic viewpoints including the No.1 Bridge in the World, a natural stone arch connecting two mountain peaks, and the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain viewpoint. The area can be accessed via the Bailong Elevator, the world's tallest outdoor elevator, or via scenic hiking trails. Yuanjiajie offers some of Zhangjiajie's most dramatic and unforgettable scenery.

Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge

The Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge is one of the most spectacular and daring engineering feats in the world, spanning a breathtaking 430 meters across a deep canyon in the Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon. Holding the Guinness World Record as the longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge in the world, this transparent bridge offers visitors an exhilarating experience as they walk 300 meters above the canyon floor on a fully transparent glass surface.

The bridge features 120 glass panels, each 5.1 cm thick and capable of supporting up to 800 people at a time. Walking across the bridge offers unobstructed views straight down to the canyon floor and across the stunning surrounding landscape. The experience is not for the faint-hearted but rewards brave visitors with unparalleled views and an unforgettable adrenaline rush. The bridge also features a stunning bungee jump platform for the truly adventurous.

Golden Whip Stream

Golden Whip Stream is one of Zhangjiajie's most beautiful and peaceful hiking trails, following a crystal-clear mountain stream through a lush, forested valley. The 7.5-kilometer trail offers a gentle walk alongside the stream, with towering sandstone cliffs rising dramatically on both sides. The stream gets its name from its golden color in the sunlight and the Golden Whip Rock, a tall, slender quartz sandstone pillar that resembles a golden whip.

The hiking trail along Golden Whip Stream is relatively flat and easy, making it accessible to most visitors. The path winds past beautiful waterfalls, natural pools, and several scenic viewpoints including the famous "Meeting of Thousand Horses" and "Lotus Peak." The area is rich in wildlife, with the chance to spot monkeys, birds, and various plant species. Golden Whip Stream offers a peaceful contrast to the dramatic mountain peaks, providing visitors with a relaxing way to experience Zhangjiajie's natural beauty at ground level.

Tianmen Mountain

Tianmen Mountain, or "Heaven's Gate Mountain," is one of Zhangjiajie's most iconic and awe-inspiring destinations, famous for its massive natural archway carved through the mountain peak. The 1,518-meter-tall mountain features a natural archway known as Tianmen Cave, which stands at an impressive 131.5 meters high, 57 meters wide, and 60 meters deep. The mountain is accessible via the world's longest cable car, offering spectacular aerial views during the ascent.

Visitors can climb the 999 steps up to the cave, known as the "Heavenly Ladder," or take the escalator system for a more comfortable ascent. At the top, a cliffside glass walkway offers breathtaking views and an exhilarating experience. The mountain also features the famous 99-turn winding road known as the "Avenue to Heaven," beautiful temples, and stunning panoramic views of the surrounding region. Tianmen Mountain offers a different but equally impressive experience compared to the quartz sandstone pillars of the main Zhangjiajie National Forest Park.

Local Food

Zhangjiajie offers a delicious blend of Hunan and local mountain cuisine, featuring fresh local ingredients, bold flavors, and unique cooking methods. The region's mountainous terrain provides an abundance of fresh wild vegetables, mushrooms, and bamboo that are featured in many local dishes.

Zhangjiajie Smoked Pork

A local specialty, Zhangjiajie smoked pork is made from free-range mountain pigs, cured with salt and spices, then smoked over pine branches and tea leaves for weeks. The result is tender, flavorful meat with a distinctive smoky aroma. It's often stir-fried with wild mountain vegetables like bracken fern or bamboo shoots, creating a perfect combination of smoky, savory flavors that represent the mountain region's culinary heritage.

Wild Mountain Vegetables

Zhangjiajie's mountainous region is rich in wild edible plants and mushrooms. Local cuisine features various wild mountain vegetables including bracken fern (juecai), wild bamboo shoots, mushroom varieties, and edible fungi. These fresh, organic ingredients are often stir-fried with garlic and chili, or served in hotpot, offering a unique taste of the region's natural bounty. The crisp texture and earthy flavors make these dishes a favorite among visitors seeking authentic mountain cuisine.

Hehua Fish (Lotus Flower Fish)

Hehua Fish is a Zhangjiajie specialty named after its delicate, flower-like presentation. The dish features whole fish, typically carp, scored and fried until golden and crispy, then served with a sweet and sour sauce. The preparation creates a beautiful "lotus flower" shape when the fish fans out during frying. The crispy exterior and tender flesh, combined with the tangy sauce, create a delicious balance of textures and flavors that's been a local favorite for generations.

Zhangjiajie Rice Noodles

Zhangjiajie is famous for its delicious rice noodles, a popular breakfast and street food option. The fresh, chewy rice noodles are served in a rich, flavorful broth with various toppings including braised pork, pickled vegetables, peanuts, and fresh herbs. Local variations include spicy versions with chili oil, as well as soup-based and stir-fried options. Many visitors consider Zhangjiajie's rice noodles among the best in Hunan Province, with a texture and flavor that's uniquely satisfying.
