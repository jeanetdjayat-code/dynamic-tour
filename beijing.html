Beijing

Explore the historical capital of China with its ancient landmarks and rich cultural heritage

Discover Beijing

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where ancient history meets modern development. With over 3,000 years of history, it's home to some of China's most iconic landmarks, including the Forbidden City, the Great Wall, and the Temple of Heaven.

Suggested Visit Time

Recommended Duration: 4-5 days

Best Time to Visit: September to October (autumn) when the weather is mild and the skies are clear, or April to May (spring) when the flowers are in bloom.

Forbidden City

The Forbidden City, also known as the Palace Museum, was the imperial palace for 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Built from 1406 to 1420, it covers an area of 720,000 square meters and contains over 9,000 rooms.

This magnificent complex is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the largest ancient wooden architectural complex in the world. Its red walls and golden roofs are iconic symbols of Chinese imperial power and architecture.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it was built to protect China from invasions. The most popular section for tourists is the Badaling section, located about 60 kilometers northwest of Beijing.

Built over several dynasties, the Great Wall is a testament to Chinese engineering and perseverance. Climbing the wall offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.

Temple of Heaven

The Temple of Heaven is a complex of religious buildings where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties prayed for good harvests. Built in the early 15th century, it's known for its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.

The main hall, the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, is a circular structure with three tiers of eaves, all covered with blue glazed tiles. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular place for locals to practice tai chi and other traditional activities.

Local Food

Beijing cuisine, also known as Jing cuisine, is known for its rich flavors and imperial heritage. It features a variety of dishes that have been influenced by the imperial court and northern Chinese traditions.

Peking Duck

A famous Beijing specialty, Peking duck is known for its crispy skin and tender meat. It's typically served with thin pancakes, scallions, and hoisin sauce.

Zhajiangmian

Wheat noodles topped with a thick sauce made from fermented soybean paste, ground pork, and vegetables. It's a popular local dish with a rich, savory flavor.

Jiaozi

Chinese dumplings filled with meat or vegetables. They can be boiled, steamed, or pan-fried and are often served with vinegar and garlic dipping sauce.

Lamb Hot Pot

A popular winter dish in Beijing, lamb hot pot involves cooking thin slices of lamb in a simmering broth at the table, along with various vegetables and noodles.
