Nanjing

Explore the ancient capital with its rich history and cultural heritage

Discover Nanjing

Nanjing, the former capital of China, is a city with a rich history spanning over 2,500 years. It served as the capital for several dynasties and is home to numerous historical landmarks, including the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, Nanjing City Wall, and the Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum.

Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum is the tomb of the first emperor of the Ming Dynasty, Zhu Yuanzhang, and his empress. Built between 1381 and 1431, it's a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its grand architecture and beautiful natural surroundings.

The mausoleum complex includes a long spirit way lined with stone statues of animals and officials, leading to the tomb itself. It's set in a scenic area with pine trees and lakes, making it a popular place for both history buffs and nature lovers.

Nanjing City Wall

The Nanjing City Wall is one of the best-preserved ancient city walls in China. Built during the Ming Dynasty, it stretches for over 35 kilometers and is known for its massive size and impressive construction.

Visitors can walk along sections of the wall, enjoying panoramic views of the city and the surrounding landscape. The wall features numerous gates, watchtowers, and fortifications, providing a glimpse into ancient Chinese military architecture.

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

The Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum is the final resting place of Sun Yat-sen, the founding father of the Republic of China. Built between 1926 and 1929, it's located on the southern slope of Purple Mountain and is known for its grand architecture and beautiful surroundings.

The mausoleum complex includes a series of steps leading up to the main hall, where Sun Yat-sen's sarcophagus is located. The area is surrounded by pine trees and offers panoramic views of Nanjing city.

Confucius Temple

The Confucius Temple, also known as the Fuzimiao, is a complex of traditional buildings dedicated to Confucius. It's located in the Qinhuai District, along the banks of the Qinhuai River, and is known for its traditional architecture and vibrant night scenes.

The temple complex includes a series of halls, pavilions, and gardens, as well as a bustling shopping area with traditional shops and restaurants. At night, the area is illuminated with red lanterns, creating a magical atmosphere.

Local Food

Nanjing cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique dishes, influenced by its history as a former capital. It features a variety of snacks and dishes that reflect the city's rich culinary heritage.

Salted Duck

Nanjing salted duck is a famous local specialty. It's made by salting and brining duck, then boiling it until tender. The result is a flavorful, tender duck with a crispy skin.

Xiaolongbao

These steamed dumplings are filled with juicy meat and broth. Nanjing's version of xiaolongbao has its own unique flavor, often with a slightly sweet taste.

Sesame Cake

This traditional snack is made with flour, sesame seeds, and various fillings. It's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, with a sweet or savory flavor depending on the filling.

Rice Porridge

Nanjing-style rice porridge is a popular breakfast dish. It's often served with various side dishes, such as pickles, preserved eggs, and tofu.
